## Executive Summary

The local-first AI assistant market is emerging rapidly. Privacy concerns with cloud AI have created demand for alternatives that run entirely on-user hardware. Micro Rush is well-positioned to capture early adopters.

## Market Opportunity

### Target Users
1. **Privacy-conscious professionals** — Doctors, lawyers, executives handling sensitive data
2. **Developers and technical users** — Self-hosters, open-source enthusiasts
3. **Power users** — Want AI that's always available, works offline

### Market Size
- Global AI personal assistant market: ~$10B by 2030 (CAGR 22%)
- Privacy-focused segment: Emerging, estimated $500M by 2025

### Why Now?
- LLMs are good enough for personal use (Llama 3, Mistral)
- Hardware (M3 Mac, modern laptops) can run models locally
- Privacy backlash against cloud AI is growing

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Local-First is the Moat
Cloud AI companies will always have trust issues. Local processing is the only true privacy guarantee.

### 2. Productivity > Entertainment
Users want AI that helps them *do* things:
- Schedule management
- Document processing
- Email/slack summarization

### 3. Memory is the Killer Feature
Generic AI forgets everything. Persistent memory that improves over time is what makes AI feel "personal."

### 4. Plugin/Skill Ecosystem
OpenAI's GPT Store shows plugins are valuable. A well-designed skill system will differentiate Micro Rush.
